# Client Admin Demo Walkthrough

Use this as a 5-minute sales demo.

## 1. Open with the pain

Say:

> Most client onboarding gets messy because the business wins the client, then the admin starts living across email, WhatsApp, documents, folders, payments, and staff memory.

Ask:

- What happens after a client says yes?
- Who sends the welcome message?
- Where do required documents go?
- How do you know what is still missing?
- Who confirms agreement/payment/folder setup?
- How do you know a client is ready to start?

## 2. Show the dashboard

Open `demo-dashboard.html`.

Point out:

- clients onboarding
- clients waiting for documents
- agreement/payment pending
- ready-to-start clients
- clients activated this week

Say:

> The first goal is to make onboarding visible. Every new client needs a status, owner, document checklist, and next action.

## 3. Show the onboarding tracker

Explain each example:

- Lerato Naidoo: welcome sent, documents requested, but key items missing.
- Thabo Mokoena: ready to start, just needs kickoff scheduled.
- Pieter Jacobs: waiting for missing business details.
- Ayesha Khan: documents received but agreement/payment pending.
- Chris Botha: onboarding complete and active.

Say:

> This is how we stop new clients from being stuck because nobody knows what is missing.

## 4. Show the document checklist

Open `document-checklist.csv`.

Say:

> The document checklist is where the admin becomes controllable. It shows exactly what is required, what is missing, who owns it, and when to follow up.

## 5. Show the weekly report

Open `filled-weekly-report.md`.

Say:

> At the end of the week, the owner can see how many clients were added, activated, stuck, waiting for documents, or blocked by agreement/payment issues.

## 6. Explain what setup includes

Say:

> We install the onboarding tracker, document checklist, folder structure, welcome templates, missing-document follow-ups, internal handover checklist, and weekly onboarding report. We start manual and controlled. Once the workflow is working, we can add forms, reminders, and light automation.

## 7. Close

Say:

> The best next step is to run this against your last 5–10 new clients or your current onboarding process. We will show where clients get stuck and what the first system should track.

## Common objections

### “We already have folders.”

Response:

> Good. We are not replacing folders. We are making sure every client has the correct folder, documents, status, and handover trail.

### “We already send welcome emails.”

Response:

> That helps. We will make the welcome process trackable so you know who received it, what is missing, and what must happen next.

### “Is this project management software?”

Response:

> No. It is a practical client admin system for onboarding, documents, agreements, payments, folders, and handovers.

### “Will automation chase clients automatically?”

Response:

> Not at the start. The first version prepares and tracks follow-ups. Your team approves and sends communication until the workflow is trusted.
